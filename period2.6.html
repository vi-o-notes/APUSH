How was slave labor distributed throughout the British colonies?  In the far north in New England, there were few enslaved people, as the farms were smaller and there was less of a need for labor there. In the large port cities like New York, there were many enslaved people because they were used as dock workers and many other tasks that needed a large amount of labor to conduct. In the southern colonies there were many more slaves because of the large farms and large labor needs all throughout those areas and not just a few cities.
